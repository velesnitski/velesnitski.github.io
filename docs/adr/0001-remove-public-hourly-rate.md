# 0001 — Remove public hourly rate from header

**Date:** 2026-06-30
**Status:** Accepted

## Context

The landing header advertised a public consulting rate: *"My typical consulting and development rates are 80$+ per hour… reductions and bespoke package rates are open for discussion."*

Problems:

- **Low anchor.** $80+/hr reads as senior-developer pricing, not Head of Engineering / CTO-level. It caps perceived value and gives counterparties a number to negotiate *down* from.
- **Colleague / employer visibility.** The page is public and indexed. A stated side-consulting rate signals moonlighting, invites questions about focus, and can be used against me in internal comp discussions.
- **No pricing flexibility.** A single public number prevents charging different clients differently (US/EU vs. smaller engagements).
- **Status.** Senior people don't post hourly rates publicly; they signal "by introduction / let's talk." Removing the number raises standing rather than lowering it.

## Decision

Removed the rate paragraph. Replaced with an understated tagline: **"Humble Computer Scientist."**

Pricing is discussed privately, per client. Credibility is carried by the linked CV, GitHub, and LinkedIn — not by a price on the page.

## Consequences

- The site no longer reveals any pricing; positioning relies on the linked profiles.
- Rate conversations move off the page and into private, per-client context.
- If lead generation is ever needed, add a soft **"available for select advisory — by introduction"** CTA — **not** a public number. Supersede this record if that changes.
